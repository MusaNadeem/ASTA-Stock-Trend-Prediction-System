Model Architecture Insights

Understand how the AI prediction engine works
ASTA

How ASTA Works

Adaptive Sparse Temporal Attention replaces dense O(T²) attention with three candidate selection strategies:

  • Local: The last k timesteps (recency bias)
  • Log-sparse: Logarithmically spaced historical points
  • Volatility: The highest-variance periods

These are merged into a sparse attention mask, reducing computation to approximately O(T log T × d).

O(T log T × d) vs O(T² × d)
TAPE

Trend-Aware Encoding

Standard positional encoding assigns positions 0…T without market context. TAPE modifies this by:

  • Momentum modulation: Scales encoding by rolling price momentum
  • Volatility adjustment: Reduces position weights in high-variance periods
  • Regime awareness: The model "knows" if the market is trending or ranging

This helps the Transformer orient its attention relative to market phase rather than raw sequence position.

MHVP

Multi-Horizon Voting

Three parallel classification heads predict trend for different time horizons:

  • Short-term head: 1-step ahead prediction
  • Mid-term head: 3-step ahead prediction
  • Long-term head: 7-step ahead prediction

Learned vote weights merge these into a single consensus. The weights are trained jointly via cross-entropy loss on each head plus a combined loss on the weighted vote target.

3-head ensemble → weighted consensus
Regime

Market Regime Detection

The system computes market regime from the price series and volatility scores:

  • Bull market: Positive trend slope, lower volatility, upward momentum
  • Bear market: Negative trend slope, elevated volatility, downward momentum
  • Sideways market: Near-zero slope, mixed volatility, no clear direction

The regime badge updates on every data load and influences how the AI model weights its outputs.
